You might expect that the $100,000 watches from Trump would be delivered from Florida, where the former U.S. President resides, as businesses are generally managed from the owner's location. However, the Trump watches are managed through a Wyoming LLC, a type of company that is increasingly popular in the Cowboy State.

Low taxes, privacy, targetted advertising and more recently an ambition of leading the crypto market are some of the reasons for this rise. We dive into OpenCorporates data to illustrate the trend.

2023: Wyoming outdoes Delaware in incorporations per capita

Last year, the Cowboy State recorded a historical 42% increase in incorporations, up to 378 per capita. In the rest of the states, only Montana has ever recorded over 50 incorporations per capita.

In 2023, Wyoming surpassed Delaware for the second time in the number of incorporations or new companies per capita (per 1,000 adults, according to census data).

For many years, Delaware was the indisputable first destination for new incorporations, and it is the domicile of choice of over 67 percent of Fortune 500 companies, according to Delaware’s Division of Corporations.

But Wyoming has recently dethroned the First State, tripling incorporations per capita over the last five years. The average of new companies created last year across the US was 11, not including Wyoming, Delaware and Puerto Rico.

This growth is due to the increasing popularity of one particular kind of entity: Limited Liability Companies. Today, LLCs count for over 75% of all companies registered in Wyoming.

Wyoming LLCs: an unparalleled rising in the last decade

This company type, easy to set up from anywhere in the world, free of corporate income tax and unregulated in Wyoming, has risen in the last twenty years and skyrocketed in the last decade.

In 2016, Wyoming LLCs experienced a significant turning point, with incorporations increasing by nearly 31% compared to the previous year—a trend that has continued since then. Around the same time, the Tax Foundation, which each year compares the tax climate among states, ranking those with lower taxes more favorably, began to place Wyoming at its top, where it has stayed ever since.

While Wyoming started to attract people to set up LLCs in their state, other types of companies have not gained the same popularity and have remained at levels similar to the average of other states. This has led to an abnormally high number of LLCs per capita in Wyoming.

Cryptocurrency and cyberattacks

Wyoming has been trying hard to become a crypto hub. In March this year, the state passed a law for Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs), which sets them apart from other types of companies, such as LLCs. Wyoming has adopted about 30 new crypto-friendly or crypto-permissive laws since 2019 and it is preparing to become the first state to have a state-backed cryptocurrency.

Anonymity and low costs are two of the qualities attracting cybercriminals to Wyoming. In the last quarter of 2023, digital defenders flagged about a handful of high-profile hackings that involved Wyoming LLCs, according to Reuters. In one of them, the International Press Institute (IPI) website was knocked down in September 2023, just after the organisation published how cyberattacks are a threat to Hungarian independent outlets. The organisation took 10 days to restore their site.
